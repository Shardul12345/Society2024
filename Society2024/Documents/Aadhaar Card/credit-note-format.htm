What is a Credit Note?

A credit note is a document acknowledging sales returns that the seller issues to the buyer. The customer can use the credit note in the future to offset future purchases. A credit memo is also known as a credit memorandum. It implies that a buyer owes a seller less than their previous invoice. You can create and send credit notes free using a professional credit note format.

It serves as the basis for the sales return journal. A supplier can issue a credit note if there is a reduction in the taxable value of the goods supplied. Although it is not a refund, it is a way for customers to buy products later without paying for them. The credit note is an acknowledgment of the debit note raised by the buyer.

Why is a Credit Note Used?

A credit note is permitted by Section 34(1) of the CGST Act when an issued tax invoice needs amendment to reduce tax liability. The uses of credit notes are as follows:

  • When the buyer returns a sale due to quality issues, service rejection, or damaged goods.
  • If the seller has collected a higher price or the buyer has erroneously paid more than the invoiced price.
  • Sellers may issue credit notes as post-sale discounts instead of refunds for goods returned.
  • When the customer receives less quantity of goods than mentioned in the tax invoice.
  • In any such situation, the buyer exchanges his purchase for other items available during the sale.

When Should Credit Notes Be Issued?

Section 34(2) of the CGST Act, 2017 states the time limit to issue credit notes. As per the act, a supplier can give a credit note on or before 30th September. Or before the date of filing of the annual return, whichever is earlier.

When a supplier/business needs to cancel an already-sent invoice, they issue a credit note. Companies can create professional credit notes using the Vyapar credit note formats.

The primary reason to give a credit note is to keep accounting records straight. You can not delete or edit an invoice once it is issued. But, the credit notes let you modify the invoice amount without deleting the invoice.

Now you must be wondering why you can’t delete invoices. The simple answer is that it is illegal to delete the invoice. You can not delete an invoice once issued according to the law. The authorities monitor and regulate these actions very strictly to protect consumer interests.

However, there are certain situations where it is necessary to cancel an invoice. The cancellation of an invoice requires the issuance of a credit memo. After all, invoices are legally binding documents.

Moreover, a credit memo allows customers to use their funds as they deem fit. Credit notes do not have to be linked to invoices. They can be issued separately to a customer.

Why do You Need a Credit Note?

Let’s understand why a credit note is required through an example. A seller creates an invoice for the goods or services sold to a customer. But if the customer observes some quality issues later, he can cancel/modify the purchase. The customer will return the goods or ask for an exchange and send a debit note.

Now, the supplier will accept and prepare a credit note using a free credit note format. It is done to acknowledge the buyer. To prepare a credit note professionally, the supplier can use various formats made available by Vyapar.

Contents of Credit Note:

A professionally created credit note includes the products, quantities, and prices for products or services the buyer returns. The supplier can issue a credit note in the event of defective goods, errors, or allowances.

Under any law, there is no prescribed credit note format. However, the supplier can include the following information in a credit note.
1. Name and address of the business.
2. GSTIN of the supplier.
3. Document type, whether debit or credit note.
4. Unique serial number not exceeding 16 characters.
5.Date of issue of the credit note.
6. The total taxable value of supply, rate, tax, and tax credit.
7. Supplier or his authorized representative’s signature.

Using a credit memo will reduce the buyer’s obligation to pay the previously issued invoice. The credit memo usually refers to the original invoice and states the issue. It may include a PO #, a date, a billing address, a shipping address, and payment terms. Further, it can have a list of products with quantities and prices.

Credit Note vs. Debit Note

Major differences between credit and debit notes are as listed below:

A debit note or debit memo is a formal request for a credit note. The buyer issues them to the seller. In contrast, a receipt that the seller sends to the buyer informing the customer about the credit made to his account is a credit memo.

Debit notes are issued when a customer or buyer returns the goods/services to the seller. In exchange, the credit notes are given when a seller undercharges an agreement or delivers extra goods.

The debit note is prepared in blue ink, whereas the credit note is in red ink. The supplier issues a credit note to the buyer in exchange for a debit note.

A debit note represents a positive amount, whereas a credit note represents a negative amount. It means that the debit note notifies the reduction in account receivables. Meanwhile, a credit note reduces account payables.

A debit note is a basis for entries in the purchase return book. Meanwhile, a credit note is a basis for the sales return book. So, a debit note reduces purchase accounts, whereas a credit note reduces sales accounts.

What is included in a credit note?

The credit note must include:

-> Customer or order reference number.
-> Payment terms and conditions
-> Contact details of a customer
-> Reason for issuing a credit note.

Is the credit note a refund?

The credit note is like a voucher that the customer can only use at the shop, chain of stores, or business that issued the credit note. So, unlike a refund, it is not providing the cash in hand. However, if the client has not paid, it eliminates their liability to pay for the goods or services.mstance.

What is the main difference between a credit note and an invoice?

Businesses issue invoices to customers as a way of billing for services they provide. A credit note is offered to customers for cancellation or modification of goods or services.

Why do we issue credit notes?

An invoice error, a damaged or incorrect item, a canceled purchase, or any other circumstance can be grounds for a credit note. It indicates the return of funds to the buyer. They can use them to get other goods or services.

Is e-invoice mandatory for credit notes?
